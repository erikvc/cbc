Church Clerk - Sis. Betty Harris

The clerk shall be responsible for keeping suitable records of all official actions of the church, except as otherwise provided in the by-laws. He/She shall be responsible for keeping accurate records of all business meeting transactions of the church. The clerk shall be responsible for keeping a register of names and address of all members, with dates of admission, dismissals, death, together with a record of baptisms and the dedication of children. L The clerk shall preserve on file all communications and written official reports, and give required notices of all meetings where notice is necessary, as indicated in the by-laws. The clerk shall be responsible for preparing annual letters of the church to the various associations, state and national conventions, as required. He/She shall issue letters of dismissal voted by the church and write letters of transfer of membership, prepare condolences, prepare written reports of the church, and keep and accurate history of the church.

Deacon - Deacon Kenneth Adams

The Deacon Ministry demonstrates spiritual leadership by serving the Pastor and congregation; and, by supporting the pastoral vision through evangelism, stewardship and discipleship. The ministry promotes harmony among - and encourages support from - the congregation. Spiritual growth of the overall membership is a priority. Sick and shut-in visitations, ongoing membership interaction, baptismal arrangements and communion service are some of the responsibilities conducted by the spiritual supporters of the congregation and Pastor. In keeping with the meaning of the work and practices in the New Testament, Deacons are to be servants of the church. They shall in the vacancy of a Pastor, be the administrators of the business and general work of the church. The Chairman of the Deacons shall serve as an advisory member to all organizations, departments and committees of the church.

Finance - Deacon Johnnie Williams

The Finance Ministry is responsible for the finances of the church including but not limited to counting each offering, depositing all monies received, budgeting, preparing monthly financial reports, handling all banking issues. This ministry will include a leader, secretary treasurer and offering counters.


Finance Committee

The Finance Committee develops and recommends an overall unified church budget. It advises and recommends the use of sound principles of financial management. The finance committee shall be comprised of not more than 9 (nine) nor less than 5 (five) members. The Chairman of the Finance Committee shall be elected by the church at the annual business meeting. The additional officers of the Finance committee shall be elected by the Committee itself. The Finance Committee shall have the authority to act on all financial policies effecting the church, except that it shall not usurp the powers of the Board of Trustees with respect to its non-delegable authorities, i.e. fixing of compensation, or those matters expressly prohibited by law. The Pastor may appoint a replacement, if a vacancy occurs on the Finance Committee, until an election can be held. The Finance Committee works with the Treasurer in the preparation and presentation to the church the required reports regarding the financial affairs of the church.

The ministry maintains an adequate inventory of kitchen supplies and ensures that the standards of the Health Department are achieved in the Kitchen and Fellowship Hall. It is essential to understand as a Greeter, they are the first persons that visitors and members see when they enter the church door, they have to always show the love of Christ. It is their responsibility to let visitors and members know that we humbly appreciate them fellowshipping with us. Treasurer - Sis. Teresa Young

The Church Treasurer is responsible for the proper receipt, accounting and disbursement of church funds within policies established by the church for adequate financial control. The Church Treasurer shall be elected as the custodian of all monies of the church and shall deposit all monies it receives from the Finance Committee, in the name of Central Baptist Church in such bank(s) as the church may designate. The Treasurer shall keep, at all times, an itemized account of all receipts and disbursements and shall render a monthly and annually written report of this account to the church. The treasurer’s report shall be audited annually by an auditing committee or public accountant. The treasurer shall be bonded, the church paying for the bond. Upon completion of the audit at the end of the fiscal year and its acceptance and approval by the church, the records shall be delivered by the treasurer to the Church Clerk, who shall keep and preserve the account as a part of the permanent records of the church. The Church Treasurer shall prepare financial statements monthly and present to the church body at the regular business meeting. The treasurer prepares an annual financial statement at the end of the fiscal year, presenting this report at the annual business meeting.
